022026

Binomial Pricing Model & Black–Scholes Convergence

Implemented an N-step Cox–Ross–Rubinstein binomial model for European calls and puts, and studied how its prices converge to the Black–Scholes closed-form benchmark as the number of time steps increases.

  • Calculated risk-neutral probabilities and added no-arbitrage checks.
  • Used backward induction to value options at each node.
  • Compared binomial prices against Black–Scholes and graphed convergence and absolute pricing error.

  • NumPy
  • matplotlib
  • Option pricing

032025

SPY Moving-Average Strategy Backtest

Built a vectorized backtest of moving-average crossover strategies on SPY and tested more than 200 short- and long-window combinations against buy-and-hold.

  • Computed CAGR, annualized volatility, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown for each strategy.
  • Shifted signals by one session to avoid look-ahead bias, then ran coarse and refined parameter searches.
  • Found some window combinations improved risk-adjusted performance and reduced drawdown, while generally capturing less total upside than buy-and-hold.

  • pandas
  • NumPy
  • matplotlib
  • yfinance
  • Backtesting
  • Time-series analysis

042025

Black–Scholes Taylor-Series Approximation

Built Taylor-series approximations of the Black–Scholes–Merton European option price and tested how pricing, Delta, and Gamma errors grow as the stock price moves away from the expansion point.

  • Compared third- and fourth-order Taylor approximations against the exact Black–Scholes formula.
  • Examined price, Delta, and Gamma error across a sweep of stock prices, and measured runtime versus the closed-form formula.
